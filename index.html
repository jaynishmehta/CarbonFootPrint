WHAT IS CARBON FOOTPRINT ?
A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases, specifically carbon dioxide and other carbon compounds, that are released into the atmosphere as a result of human activities. These activities include things like driving a car, using electricity, manufacturing goods, and even the food we consume. The carbon footprint is often measured in units of carbon dioxide equivalent (CO2) and is a way to assess the impact of individuals, organizations, or products on climate change by quantifying their contribution to the accumulation of greenhouse gases in the Earth's atmosphere. The goal is to reduce carbon footprints to mitigate the negative effects of climate change.
